Tone of Voice

How We Communicate

Our voice is professional yet warm — never cold, never corporate. We make the complex feel manageable and the journey feel supported. Updated for today: concise, direct, and confident without sacrificing genuine human care.

Use

  • Dream, family, community, together, home
  • Trusted, reliable, experienced, proven
  • Clear, plain-language explanations
  • Encouraging, forward-looking language
  • Confident — grounded in 37 years of results

Avoid

  • Unexplained mortgage jargon
  • Impersonal or institutional language
  • Overly promotional or salesy tone
  • Urgency tactics or pressure language
  • Generic copy that could belong to anyone
Visual Identity

Logo

Wordmark "Land Home" in a classic serif script — refined and trustworthy. "FINANCIAL SERVICES, INC." in spaced all-caps beneath.
Icon A warm amber/gold house nestled between the two words — the bridge between land and home. The brand's most distinctive mark.
Feel Heritage meets sharp precision. The logo is the anchor — timeless script meets modern clarity in all applications.
Usage Maintain clear space at all times. Do not alter colors, stretch, or apply filters. On dark surfaces, use the full-color version.

Amber Gold remains the soul of the brand — the house icon, the call to action, the earned accent. The updated palette places it against deep navy and charcoal backgrounds, letting it glow with earned presence rather than compete for space. Glass surfaces with backdrop blur replace flat white panels — translucent, layered, and alive.

Typography

Role Style Application

Body Copy Clean, readable sans-serif All written communications, web, email

Visual Identity

Imagery

Real Families Warm Natural Light Diverse Representation All Home Types Authentic Moments No Stock Clichés

Imagery should feel genuine and aspirational — not staged. Feature people of all backgrounds in real home environments: traditional, manufactured, renovated, newly built. Warm, natural light reinforces the brand's welcoming character. On dark digital surfaces, imagery should feel cinematic and grounded — not clinical.
